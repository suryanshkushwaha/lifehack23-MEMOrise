Why This Method Works

Visual Distinction:
- Highlighting important or key words/phrases in a text creates a visual distinction that draws attention. When studying or reviewing the material, the highlighted text stands out, making it easier to recognize and remember the crucial information.
Focus and Organization:
- By selectively highlighting specific words or phrases, you can emphasize the most relevant information within a text. This process helps you focus on the essential concepts and creates a visual organization that aids in understanding and memorization.
Retrieval Practice:
- The act of highlighting involves active engagement with the material. When you read through the text and decide which parts to highlight, you are actively processing and evaluating the information. This process of retrieval practice strengthens memory recall and retention.

Tips to Implement This Method

Main Ideas: Highlight the main ideas or central concepts of the text. These are the key takeaways or overarching themes that the author is conveying.

Supporting Details: Highlight supporting details that provide evidence, examples, or explanations for the main ideas. These details help reinforce and illustrate the main points.
Definitions and Terminology: Highlight important definitions, technical terms, or vocabulary that are critical to understanding the subject matter.
Key Phrases or Keywords: Highlight specific phrases or keywords that encapsulate important information or serve as cues for recall.
Headings or Subheadings: If applicable, highlight headings or subheadings that provide a structural outline of the content. These can help in organizing the information mentally.
